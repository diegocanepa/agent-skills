# Professional Communication Patterns

## Tone and Style
- **English Only**: All communications must be in English.
- **Emojis Allowed**: Use emojis to provide visual feedback and a friendly tone (e.g., ‚úÖ, üõë, üîÑ, ‚ö†Ô∏è).
- **Professional & Collaborative**: Use active voice and focus on solutions.
- **Empathetic**: Acknowledge the user's or colleague's concerns.

## Human-in-the-Loop (MITM)
- **Confirmation Required**: ALWAYS show the drafted comment to the USER and wait for confirmation before posting it to the issue/VCS.

## Response Patterns

### Responding to Questions
- **Acknowledge**: "Thanks for the question regarding [Topic]."
- **Clarify**: "If I understand correctly, you are asking about [Interpretation]."
- **Answer**: Provide a direct answer or a clear path to getting one.
- **Next Step**: "Does this clarify your doubt, or should we discuss [Alternative]?"

### Handling Blockers
- **Immediate Alert**: "BEWARE: This task is currently BLOCKED."
- **Reason**: "The blocker is [Specific Reason/Dependency]."
- **Impact**: "This delays [Milestone/Task] by approximately [Time]."
- **Action**: "To unblock, we need [Person/Resource] to [Action]."

### Communicating Delays
- **Proactive**: Don't wait for the deadline.
- **Explain**: "We are seeing a delay in [Task] due to [Technical Challenge/Context]."
- **New ETA**: "The revised expected completion date is [Date]."
- **Mitigation**: "We are [Action being taken] to minimize the impact."
